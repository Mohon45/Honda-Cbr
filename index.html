Honda CBR 300R

The 2021 Honda CBR150R has been launched in Indonesia. The updated model comes with a much more aggressive bodywork similar to the Honda CBR250RR. It also gets a more premium Showa SFF-BP inverted front fork and a rear monoshock. Interestingly, apart from providing a more sophisticated damping, the front fork isn’t preload-adjustable like the telescopic fork in its predecessor. Other than this, the bike also features a 6-speed transmission with an assist-and-slipper clutch. The assist function is claimed to reduce the lever effort by 15 percent whereas the slipper clutch prevents the rear wheel from locking up during hard downshifts. That said, the engine is still the same 149.16cc DOHC single-cylinder liquid-cooled engine as before. It also churns out the same output: 17.1PS at 9000rpm and 14.4Nm at 7000rpm.
